It's the big day, time to sign (and initial) lots of papers! It won't be long before you get the keys to your new home and can begin moving in. Here's what to expect when you close on your new home.

  • One of the first things you'll provide is a driver's license or other photo ID.
  • You'll sign a mortgage document promising to pay back your home loan.
  • Other documents you'll sign are acknowledgments, disclosures, surveys, certifications, etc.
  • You will be asked for a certified bank check for the balance of the down payment and a certified check for the closing costs.
  • You will be given a deed; copies of the appraisal, inspection reports, and termite certification and of course the keys to the property.
  • Unless you agreed in advance to delay taking possession of the home, you are now the new owner of the property.
Closing are fairly brief, usually lasting about an hour, but don't feel you have to rush through the process. Read what you are signing and ask questions if you are unsure of what you are signing, you can even bring your own real estate attorney if you wish.
